## Context

These were not one large project. They were three focused initiatives at different moments of the merchant journey: after setup, during onboarding, and before signup. The scope covered journey design, experiment concepts, UX flows, and rollout support.

The goal was simple: understand where merchants were getting stuck, design an intervention for that moment, take it live, and measure whether it helped them move further into Razorpay. The journey we were working across:

**Result: the early lift became a validation lesson.** Smart Assist initially showed 6.7% signup versus 5.9% control: a +14% signal. A later 33/33/34 split showed 4.7% versus 4.4% with overlapping confidence intervals, so there was no clear winner. Mic permission remained a major friction, with about 44% declining.

## What I would carry forward

- Growth design worked best when it reduced uncertainty at a real user decision point, not when it simply added another nudge.
- The strongest case-study moments are the decisions behind the screens: why a banner is persistent, why a reward needs re-entry, why a voice widget needs a chat fallback.
- Measurement design matters as much as visual design. Smart Assist is a useful example of how early lift can turn into sharper learning after better validation.
