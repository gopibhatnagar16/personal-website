## Context

These were not one large project. They were three focused initiatives at different moments of the merchant journey: after setup, during onboarding, and before signup.

The goal was simple: understand where merchants were getting stuck, design an intervention for that moment, take it live, and measure whether it helped them move further into Razorpay. The journey we were working across: **visitor** (compares pricing and credibility) → **signup** (has an account, may not see value yet) → **onboarding** (setup, KYC, dashboard tasks) → **activation** (starts accepting payments) → **growth** (returns, transacts again, scales usage).

## Experiment 01 — Gamification

Merchants reached payment enablement, but still lacked a clear reason to keep transacting. The OKR: increase MTU and GMV by helping payment-enabled merchants complete meaningful transactions. How might we make the next transaction visible, achievable, and worth returning for?

The journey we designed: FTUX completes → a challenge frames the next goal as eligible transactions → progress, time left, and milestone proximity stay visible → a reward moment (claim, reveal, no-reward, or completed) → dashboard, WhatsApp, and email bring the merchant back. We used challenge framing, visible progress, time pressure, and reward anticipation — and scaled only after positive reads.

<ImagePlaceholder />

**Result: 5 of 7 early experiments were positive.** The early tests generated ₹45.3 lakh incremental GMV from 595 incremental merchants. The April scale-up reported **₹22.3 crore incremental GMV**, ₹23.9 lakh incremental NR, and **1.3 lakh incremental transactions** across 18 experiments.

## Experiment 02 — Free PG for new merchants

Cost hesitation and offer invisibility could slow a new merchant before they started accepting payments. The OKR: increase new-merchant activation by giving eligible PG merchants a visible free-credit offer — ₹5 lakh in free credits for 90 days. How might we make the offer clear at every step from signup to go-live?

We mapped the offer across the whole journey rather than a single banner: introduce the benefit at **signup**, keep it visible through the **onboarding form**, tie it to progress in the **setup checklist**, keep it retrievable from the **top bar and profile menu** after go-live, and use **campaign banners** for lifecycle reminders — then simplified surfaces with engineering feedback.

<ImagePlaceholder />

**Result: credit assignment reached 95.7% success at 100% ramp.** 1,238 of 1,294 activation requests received credits successfully, while 56 expected eligibility rejections were classified correctly. This validates rollout health; revenue, GMV, and retention impact remain follow-up reads.

## Experiment 03 — Smart Assist on the pricing page

Pricing-page visitors had product, fee, fit, and trust questions a static page could not answer — but an AI widget had to prove real lift, not just look modern. The OKR: improve visitor-to-signup conversion by reducing decision uncertainty before signup.

We benchmarked Smart Assist and Spotinfo against control, improved the prompt, and designed the fallback path deliberately: visitors who blocked the microphone were routed toward chat with Ray, because voice will not work for everyone.

<ImagePlaceholder />

**Result: the early lift became a validation lesson.** Smart Assist initially showed 6.7% signup versus 5.9% control — a +14% signal. A later 33/33/34 split showed 4.7% versus 4.4% with overlapping confidence intervals, so there was no clear winner. Mic permission remained a major friction, with about 44% declining.

## What I would carry forward

- Growth design worked best when it reduced uncertainty at a real user decision point, not when it simply added another nudge.
- The strongest case-study moments are the decisions behind the screens: why a banner is persistent, why a reward needs re-entry, why a voice widget needs a chat fallback.
- Measurement design matters as much as visual design. Smart Assist is a useful example of how early lift can turn into sharper learning after better validation.
